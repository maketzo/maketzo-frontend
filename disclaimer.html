3. Past performance does not guarantee future results

This is the most important sentence on this page. Repeat it back: past performance does not guarantee future results. It applies to your own historical trades, to other traders' results, to backtests, to any chart on this website, and to any signal or outcome we ever describe.

Markets are non-stationary. A setup that worked last quarter may not work this quarter. A trader who was profitable last year may lose this year. A discipline streak that's helped you for months can break in a single session. Treat every claim of historical success — including your own — with appropriate skepticism.

4. Edge and Pulse are signals — not guarantees

Maketzo Edge and Maketzo Pulse are proprietary signals that produce outputs like "Prime", "Hold", "Exit", "Rising", and "Fading". These outputs reflect what our algorithms observe in real time. They are not predictions of future price. They are not buy or sell recommendations. They are not directionally guaranteed.

A "Prime" reading does not mean a trade will be profitable. A "Fading" reading does not mean a position should be closed immediately. You are responsible for every trading decision you make, and how you incorporate any signal into your own plan is entirely your responsibility.

Individual results vary, and reviews are not representative of typical results. Many traders lose money. Some traders profit. MAKETZO is one tool in a discipline stack — not the cause of any individual outcome.

Live activity displays ("trader joined", "session completed", etc.) are real events reflecting current user activity. They are not endorsements of results.

7. Consult a licensed advisor

If you are unsure whether trading is appropriate for you, consult a licensed financial advisor, CPA, or attorney before you begin or before making any significant decision. They can assess your specific situation in ways that no software product — including MAKETZO — ever could.

This is especially important if:
